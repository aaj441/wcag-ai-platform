## üöÄ Getting Started (30 Minutes)

### Step 1: Choose Your Target Tool (5 min)
Pick an AI tool that your target audience uses:

**For SaaS Executives:**
- ChatGPT, Notion AI, Jasper AI

**For Developers:**
- GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Replit AI

**For Enterprise:**
- Salesforce Einstein, Microsoft Copilot, Zoom AI

**For Designers:**
- Figma AI, Midjourney, Canva AI

### Step 2: Conduct Basic Testing (15 min)
You don't need expensive tools. Start with:

#### Keyboard Testing
1. Open the AI tool
2. Unplug your mouse (or don't touch it)
3. Try to navigate using only:
   - `Tab` key (move forward)
   - `Shift + Tab` (move backward)
   - `Enter` (activate buttons)
   - `Esc` (close dialogs)
4. **Document any place you get stuck**

#### Screen Reader Testing (Free)
- **Windows:** Built-in Narrator
- **Mac:** Built-in VoiceOver (Cmd+F5)
- **Chrome:** ChromeVox extension

Turn on screen reader and try to:
- Navigate the interface
- Understand button purposes
- Know when AI is responding
- **Document anything that's unclear or silent**

#### Visual Testing
- Check color contrast: [WebAIM Contrast Checker](https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/)
- Try zooming to 200% (Ctrl/Cmd +)
- **Document any text that becomes unreadable**

### Step 3: Map to WCAG Guidelines (5 min)

Match your findings to WCAG references:

| Issue Found | WCAG Guideline | Severity |
|-------------|----------------|----------|
| Can't use keyboard | 2.1.1 Keyboard | Critical |
| Get stuck (keyboard trap) | 2.1.2 No Keyboard Trap | Critical |
| Missing button labels | 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value | High |
| Poor contrast | 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) | High |
| No alt text | 1.1.1 Non-text Content | Medium |
| Confusing layout | 1.3.1 Info and Relationships | Medium |

**Use the [WCAG Quick Reference](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/quickref/) for full list**

### Step 4: Write Your Post (5 min)

Use the template from `AI_ACCESSIBILITY_TEARDOWN_TEMPLATE.md`:

```markdown
I spent [TIME] analyzing [TOOL_NAME].

It's brilliant, but it fails one critical test: **AI Accessibility**.

Here's the 30-second teardown:

‚ùå **[Violation 1]:** [Description]. (WCAG [Reference])
‚ùå **[Violation 2]:** [Description]. (WCAG [Reference])
‚ùå **[Violation 3]:** [Description]. (WCAG [Reference])

These aren't just "usability issues." They are compliance risks that open the door to ADA lawsuits and exclude over 25% of the population.

The fix? It's not about abandoning AI. It's about implementing a "Cyborg" approach:
1. Automate Scans in CI/CD
2. Human-in-the-Loop Audits
3. Build an Evidence Vault

AI is creating a new wave of legal risk for tech companies. But it's also the only scalable way to solve it.

I'm building a playbook for SaaS companies to turn this risk into an advantage. Follow me for more "Cyborg Shield" strategies.

#Accessibility #AI #WCAG #Compliance #SaaS #RiskManagement
```

## üìã Pre-Flight Checklist

Before posting, verify:

- [ ] **Tested personally** - Don't rely on screenshots or third-party claims
- [ ] **Specific WCAG references** - Always cite the guideline number
- [ ] **Date-stamped** - Tools update; note when you tested
- [ ] **Fair representation** - Focus on facts, not sensationalism
- [ ] **Solution-oriented** - Include how to fix, not just problems
- [ ] **Hashtags included** - 5-7 relevant tags maximum
- [ ] **Spell-checked** - Typos hurt credibility
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly** - Preview on phone before posting

## üé® Content Formats

### Format 1: Text-Only (Fastest)
Just the post copy. Best for:
- Breaking news (new tool launched)
- Quick observations
- Daily consistency

**Time:** 5 minutes
**Engagement:** Moderate

### Format 2: Text + Screenshot
Post copy + annotated image showing the violation. Best for:
- Visual violations (contrast, layout)
- Proof of issue
- Technical audience

**Time:** 15 minutes
**Engagement:** High

### Format 3: Carousel PDF
Multi-slide document with:
- Slide 1: Hook
- Slides 2-4: Each violation (one per slide)
- Slide 5: Solution framework
- Slide 6: Call to action

**Time:** 30 minutes
**Engagement:** Very High

**Tools:**
- Canva (free templates)
- Google Slides
- PowerPoint

### Format 4: Video Walkthrough
Screen recording showing the accessibility failure. Best for:
- Keyboard traps
- Screen reader issues
- Complex interactions

**Time:** 45 minutes
**Engagement:** Excellent

**Tools:**
- Loom (free tier)
- OBS Studio (free, open-source)
- QuickTime (Mac built-in)

## üìÖ Posting Strategy

### Frequency
**Recommended:** 2-3 posts per week
- Too little: Lose momentum
- Too much: Audience fatigue

### Best Days/Times (LinkedIn)
1. **Tuesday 9 AM** (peak engagement)
2. **Wednesday 12 PM** (lunch break scrolling)
3. **Thursday 9 AM** (second-best day)

**Avoid:**
- Monday mornings (email overload)
- Friday afternoons (checked out)
- Weekends (low B2B activity)

### Content Mix (Weekly)
- **Post 1:** AI tool teardown (broad appeal)
- **Post 2:** Industry-specific insight (niche audience)
- **Post 3:** Success story or case study (social proof)

## üí¨ Engagement Tactics

### First 2 Hours Are Critical
LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes posts with early engagement.

**Do this immediately after posting:**
1. Reply to every comment (within 15 minutes)
2. Tag 2-3 relevant people who might find it interesting
3. Share in relevant LinkedIn groups
4. Post a comment with additional context
5. Cross-post to Twitter as a thread

### Conversation Starters
Add these to your post or first comment:

- "What AI tools should I audit next? Drop suggestions below üëá"
- "Have you experienced this issue? Share your story."
- "Developers: How would you solve this violation?"
- "What's your biggest accessibility concern with AI?"

### Handle Objections
**"Accessibility is a niche concern"**
‚Üí "25% of the population has disabilities. That's 1 billion people and $13T in spending power."

**"Our users haven't complained"**
‚Üí "70% of accessibility issues are silent failures. Users abandon sites without telling you why."

**"Fixing this is too expensive"**
‚Üí "Domino's Pizza spent $8M on an accessibility lawsuit. Prevention is cheaper than remediation."

**"AI will fix this automatically"**
‚Üí "AI created these problems. AI + human oversight is the solution."

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Don't Do This:
1. **Claim legal expertise** - "This is illegal" (you're not a lawyer)
2. **Exaggerate severity** - "Everyone will sue them!" (be factual)
3. **Test only once** - Tools update; re-verify before posting
4. **Ignore updates** - If tool fixes the issue, post an update
5. **Bash the company** - Focus on product improvement, not attacks

### ‚úÖ Do This Instead:
1. **Cite WCAG guidelines** - "This violates WCAG 2.1.2"
2. **State facts** - "71% of disabled users abandon sites with chat issues (WebAIM)"
3. **Test thoroughly** - Keyboard, screen reader, visual checks
4. **Give credit** - "Update: [Tool] fixed the keyboard trap issue!"
5. **Offer solutions** - "Here's how to fix this..."

## üìû Questions?

**Common Questions:**

**Q: What if I'm not an accessibility expert?**
A: You don't need to be. Basic keyboard and screen reader testing is enough to find critical issues. Start simple, learn as you go.

**Q: What if the company responds negatively?**
A: Stay professional. Focus on helping them improve, not shaming. Most companies appreciate constructive feedback.

**Q: How do I handle disagreement about severity?**
A: Cite WCAG guidelines and user research. Stick to facts, not opinions.

**Q: Should I offer paid audits in my posts?**
A: Soft CTA only. "DM for consultation" works. Hard sales pitches hurt engagement.
