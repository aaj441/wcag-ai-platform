# Insurance Requirements - WCAG AI Platform

## Executive Summary

The WCAG AI Platform requires comprehensive insurance coverage to protect against professional liability, technology risks, and data security incidents. This document outlines mandatory and recommended insurance policies for safe business operations.

## Mandatory Coverage

### 1. Technology Errors & Omissions (E&O) Insurance

**Purpose**: Protects against claims of professional negligence, errors, or omissions in accessibility consulting services.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $2,500-$4,000
- **Coverage Limit**: $1,000,000 per claim / $2,000,000 aggregate
- **Deductible**: $2,500-$5,000
- **Retroactive Date**: Policy inception date

**Key Protections**:
- Incorrect WCAG compliance assessments
- Missed accessibility violations
- Faulty recommendations causing client harm
- Professional negligence claims
- Defense costs and legal fees

**Recommended Providers**:
- Hiscox (tech-focused)
- Chubb (comprehensive coverage)
- Hartford (small business friendly)
- CNA (professional services specialist)

### 2. Cyber Liability Insurance

**Purpose**: Covers data breaches, cyber attacks, and technology failures.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $1,500-$2,500
- **Coverage Limit**: $1,000,000
- **Deductible**: $1,000-$2,500

**Key Protections**:
- Data breach notification costs
- Credit monitoring for affected clients
- Business interruption from cyber attacks
- Ransomware and extortion coverage
- Network security liability
- Media liability (online content)

**Required Features**:
- First-party coverage (own business losses)
- Third-party coverage (client data breaches)
- Incident response team access
- Public relations crisis management
- Regulatory defense coverage

### 3. General Liability Insurance

**Purpose**: Protects against bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury claims.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $1,200-$1,500
- **Coverage Limit**: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate
- **Deductible**: $500-$1,000

**Key Protections**:
- Client office visit incidents
- Advertising injury claims
- Third-party property damage
- Personal injury claims
- Legal defense costs

### 4. Professional Liability Insurance

**Purpose**: Additional layer of protection for consulting services.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $600-$1,200
- **Coverage Limit**: $500,000-$1,000,000
- **Deductible**: $1,000-$2,500

**Key Protections**:
- Professional advice liability
- Consulting service errors
- Breach of professional duty
- Misrepresentation claims

## Recommended Additional Coverage

### 5. Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

**Purpose**: Bundles general liability and property insurance.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $1,000-$2,000
- **Coverage**: Property + Liability + Business Interruption

**Benefits**:
- Cost savings through bundling
- Simplified coverage management
- Business interruption protection

### 6. Workers' Compensation Insurance

**Purpose**: Required if hiring employees (contractors exempt).

**Coverage Details**:
- **Cost**: ~$0.50-$2.00 per $100 of payroll
- **Coverage**: Employee injuries and illnesses

**Requirement**: Mandatory in most states for businesses with employees.

### 7. Commercial Auto Insurance

**Purpose**: Required if using vehicle for business purposes.

**Coverage Details**:
- **Annual Premium**: $800-$1,500
- **Coverage**: Business-related vehicle use

## AI-Specific Considerations

### Enhanced Coverage for AI Services

Given the use of AI in accessibility assessment, consider:

1. **AI-Specific E&O Endorsements**:
   - Coverage for AI algorithm errors
   - Machine learning model liability
   - Automated decision-making protection

2. **Technology Risk Clauses**:
   - API integration failures
   - Third-party AI service disruptions
   - Training data bias claims

3. **Regulatory Compliance Coverage**:
   - AI transparency requirements
   - Algorithmic accountability laws
   - Emerging AI regulations

### Required Disclosures to Insurers

When applying for insurance, disclose:
- Use of AI/ML in service delivery
- Human oversight processes
- Quality assurance procedures
- Data security measures
- Compliance certifications

## Policy Management

### Annual Review Checklist
- [ ] Review coverage limits against revenue growth
- [ ] Update insurable value for equipment/property
- [ ] Verify cybersecurity measures meet policy requirements
- [ ] Confirm all certificates of insurance (COI) are current
- [ ] Review claims history and adjust coverage
- [ ] Shop rates with 2-3 carriers for competitive pricing

### Certificate of Insurance (COI) Management

Maintain current COIs for:
- Client contract requirements
- Subcontractor agreements
- Co-working space leases
- Professional association memberships

**Storage**: Keep digital and physical copies in secure location.

### Claims Process

**If an incident occurs**:
1. **Immediate Action**: Document incident thoroughly
2. **Notification**: Contact insurer within 24-48 hours
3. **Preservation**: Preserve all evidence and communications
4. **Legal Counsel**: Consult attorney before admitting fault
5. **Cooperation**: Fully cooperate with insurer investigation

## Provider Selection Criteria

### Evaluation Factors
1. **Financial Strength**: A.M. Best rating of A- or higher
2. **Claims Reputation**: Research claim payment history
3. **Tech Industry Experience**: Familiarity with tech E&O
4. **Customer Service**: 24/7 claims reporting
5. **Policy Features**: Cyber incident response team access

### Recommended Brokers
- **Embroker**: Tech-focused online broker
- **CoverWallet**: Small business specialist
- **Next Insurance**: Instant online quotes
- **Local Independent Broker**: Personalized service

## Compliance Documentation

### Required Records
- Policy declarations pages
- Coverage certificates
- Claims correspondence
- Premium payment receipts
- Annual policy renewal notices

**Retention Period**: 7 years minimum (IRS requirement)

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### To Maintain Insurability
1. Implement robust data security practices
2. Maintain comprehensive audit trails
3. Use clear contract language and disclaimers
4. Conduct regular quality assurance reviews
5. Stay current with industry certifications
6. Document all client communications
7. Implement incident response plan

### Red Flags That Increase Premiums
- Prior claims history
- Inadequate cybersecurity measures
- Lack of professional certifications
- Poor financial health
- High-risk client industries
- International operations
