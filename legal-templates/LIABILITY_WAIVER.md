The WCAG AI Platform Service performs **automated accessibility scanning only**. Client understands and agrees that:

1. **Incomplete Coverage**: Automated tools cannot detect all accessibility issues. Some violations may be missed, and the Service may report false positives.

2. **No Legal Guarantee**: Passing automated scans does NOT guarantee compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Section 508, WCAG standards, or any other legal accessibility requirements.

3. **Manual Testing Required**: Comprehensive accessibility assessment requires manual testing with assistive technologies (screen readers, voice control, etc.) by qualified accessibility professionals.

4. **Dynamic Content Limitations**: JavaScript-rendered content, third-party embeds, and dynamic functionality may be partially or completely unavailable to automated scanners.

5. **Contextual Issues**: Many accessibility issues are contextual and cannot be detected by automated analysis. Examples include:
   - Semantic meaning issues
   - Focus management in interactive widgets
   - Complex table structures
   - Form field relationships
   - Page structure and navigation intent

## SPECIFIC DISCLAIMERS

### 1. ADA/Section 508 Compliance

**WCAG AI Platform is NOT a guarantee of ADA or Section 508 compliance.**

- The ADA and Section 508 require both WCAG 2.1 compliance AND overall accessibility
- Automated testing cannot assess overall accessibility
- Courts have found WCAG compliance alone to be insufficient for ADA compliance
- "WCAG Compliant" does not mean "ADA Compliant" or "Lawsuit Proof"

### 2. WCAG Levels

**The Service scans against WCAG standards but does not guarantee any WCAG level.**

- Passing automated scanning does NOT mean achieving WCAG Level A, AA, or AAA
- Some WCAG criteria require manual assessment

### 3. Industry Standards

**Results are based on industry-standard automated tools but are not error-proof.**

Tools used include axe-core, Pa11y, and WAVE. These are well-regarded but have limitations:
